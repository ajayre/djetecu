This area on the Aux Board is where the injector pulse is modified by bias inputs from the Speed Correction, Acceleration Enrichmentt, and Coolant Temperature Compensation circuits.

Note the numerous (17) elevated resistors seen in the center. These are "switched on test" resistors where tuning can be performed by replacing resistors with different values. SOT resistors are depicted in bold throughout this schematic.

Acceleration Enrichment
Aug 13, 2020, 1:17:27 AM(edited: Aug 13, 2020, 1:17:54 AM)
This area of the Aux Board takes a signal from the TPS via ECU terminals 9 and 20 through Aux leads 3 and 5 as alternating HI - LO as the throttle is advanced. The Acceleration Enrichment circuit uses this step function to enrich the mixture based on driver demand from the right foot.

An idle signal from the TPS (terminal 17 via Aux 2) shuts down this circuit.

Fuel Pump Control
Aug 13, 2020, 1:14:26 AM(edited: Aug 13, 2020, 1:15:11 AM)
This circuit delivers a switched ground to ECU terminal 19 which is wired to terminal 85 of the Fuel Pump Relay under two conditions:

1. ECU powered without a speed signal (100 series Aux Board) > C400 provides switched ground for 1.5 seconds to pressurize fuel loop for start.

2. ECU powered with a speed signal (above 300 RPM), continuous ground delivered through T401 until engine RPM drops or ECU is powered off.

Injector Drivers
Aug 13, 2020, 1:12:15 AM(edited: Aug 13, 2020, 1:13:28 AM)
The Injector Drivers are the heavy components along the right edge and upper edge of this picture. This is actually the lower and left sides of the ECU when it is mounted - easily identified by the 2 large heat sinks mounted on those sides. Injector pulses from the drivers are output to the ECU terminals shown:

3 > I > 1 & 5
4 > II > 4 & 8
5 > III > 6 & 3
6 > IV > 7 & 2

Note the correlation to the M117 firing order

1-5-4-8-6-3-7-2

This part of the ECU draws power from terminal 24. The rest of the ECU draws power from terminal 16.
